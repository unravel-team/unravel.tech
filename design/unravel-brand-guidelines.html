Gradients

Important: Unravel's brand identity is built on solid colors, not gradients. The logo and primary design elements use flat, vibrant colors for maximum clarity and impact.

When to Use Gradients

Use gradients sparingly and only for:

  • Subtle background treatments for digital applications
  • Hover states on interactive elements
  • Depth effects on cards or elevated surfaces
  • Decorative backgrounds that don't compete with content

Usage Guidelines

‚úì Do
  • Use green and blue together for the logo
  • Apply green underlines for emphasis
  • Use cream backgrounds for main layouts
  • Maintain clear hierarchy with typography
  • Use the tagline with ELITE underlined
  • Keep icon styles consistent and simple
  • Prefer solid colors over gradients
  • Use subtle gradients only for background depth
‚úó Don't
  • Don't change the logo colors
  • Don't use green and blue as gradients together
  • Don't place the logo on busy backgrounds
  • Don't use more than two accent colors per design
  • Don't modify the tagline wording
  • Don't use pure white (#FFFFFF) for main backgrounds
  • Don't create vibrant or saturated gradients
  • Don't blend brand colors directly in gradients
